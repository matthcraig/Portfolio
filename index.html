About Me

I came to coding very recently, after years of working in the arts - a notably subjective and highly saturated field. I began casually inspecting webpages code, then shortly after became obsessed with watching David Malan's CS50 lectures at Harvard, even tackling some of the problem sets for that course (all in C). At that point, the allure of solving problems with a type of "language" was my real interest. Since 2012, I've worked as an assistant Adjunct Professor of Art Foundations and Painting, a small business owner, and a practicing artist.

My Background

I was born and raised in Portland Oregon. My father, now deceased, made his living as a small business owner and jazz musician. My mother, to this day is the principal Harpist in the Oregon Symphony, in Portland, OR. Early on, I studied music, later taking a keen interest in visual arts, which I pursued as an Undergraduate at University of Oregon, where I received my BFA Painting degree in 2003. I later went on to receive a MFA degree at the storied Tyler Shcool of Art in Philadelphia, PA, where I now live.
